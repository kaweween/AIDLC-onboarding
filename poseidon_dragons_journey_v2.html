The problem 03 / 12

140+ athletes, 3 disconnected tools, zero structure. Officers doing data entry by hand in Messenger group chats.
📱 Messenger
Training schedules, attendance polls, announcements, payment screenshots — all buried in one group chat with no structure or history
📊 Google Sheets
Member roster updated manually by officers. No validation, no role control. Anyone with the link could edit anything.
📁 Google Drive
Payment screenshots, ID photos, docs scattered in folders with zero link to member records

No change history, no payment confirmation system, no attendance records beyond chat messages going back months

Domain knowledge wins 08 / 12
The corrections that made it real
Every correction Kareen made became a documented architectural decision. This is the difference between a generic app and one that actually fits a dragonboat team.
  • K
    Tailwind v4 broke the build — caught immediately. Pinned to v3. Now documented and enforced in all generation specs.
  • K
    Dragonboat positions — generic labels corrected to actual positions: Bow/Left, Stroke/Right, Ambidextrous, Drummer, Helm. AI didn't know dragonboat.
  • K
    Login freeze — navigate() during render caused infinite loop. Fixed with useEffect pattern, now a hard NFR in all specs.
  • K
    Critical architecture bug — discovered that member docs had random Firestore IDs but auth resolves by UID. Entire Member Onboarding epic was born from this catch.
  • K
    Race categories — AI didn't know the Open/Mixed/Women/Men × Standard/Short × Premier/U24/U18/Masters × distance matrix. Kareen specified it exactly.
  • K
    Gemini over Anthropic — cost decision made mid-development. Switched AI providers without changing callers. Practical product ownership.
  • K
    Guest & fee automation — entire epics added after MVP because Kareen knew the operational gaps that data entry alone doesn't reveal.
"AI generates. The person with domain knowledge makes it accurate, useful, and real."
